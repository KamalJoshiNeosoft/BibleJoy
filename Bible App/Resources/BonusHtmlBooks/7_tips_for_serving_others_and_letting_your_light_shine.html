7 tips for serving others and letting your light shine

 

Phillipians 2:4 says, ¨Let each of you look not only to his own interests, but also to the interests of others.¨

 

Finding ways to serve others can be a very rewarding experience.  Following these seven tips will make it easier for you to build relationships and let your light shine.

 

  1. Use your time.Finding time to do the many tasks our days consist of can be hard.  Look for someone who has even less time than you do and ask how you can help.  This could be a single mom who needs help running errands.  Or, it might be a family with a new baby.  Regardless of who it is, using your time will surely be a reflection of Galatians 6:2:  ¨Bear one another's burdens, and so fulfill the law of Christ.¨

 

  1. Use your talents.  We all have talents that allow us to help others.  Are you good at baking?  Bake some treats and make other people´s mealtime a bit easier.  Teaching an art class at a local nursing home or reading to residents can be great ways to use various talents.  With concerns about visitation still looming, phone calls can be another great way to connect with the talents you have. 

 

  1. Ask how you can help.  Sometimes people need help, but may be reluctant to ask.  Reach out and ask others how you can help them.  Even if they do not need help, your question will serve as a willingness to support them in future endeavors. 

 

  1. Collect donations.  Have a donation drive for a local charity or organization.  This service project can be a great way to let someone know you are thinking of them.  The donated items can come with a simple note about God's blessings.  It might say:  I hope these items brighten your day and remind you of God's love.

 

  1. Serve at a local shelter.  Finding a local shelter or other organization that needs volunteers is a great way to make connections and make a difference.  Never underestimate how much your time can impact another person.

 

  1. Teach a class.  A local shelter may need volunteers to teach a class at a local shelter, recovery center, or some other entity.  Pray to God for direction in finding where He wants you to serve. 

 

  1. Invite guests to come over.  Open up your home and show hospitality to others.  This service will allow them to have a closer connection with you. 

 

¨In the same way, let your light shine before others, so that they may see your good works and give glory to your Father who is in heaven.¨  Matthew 5:16.
