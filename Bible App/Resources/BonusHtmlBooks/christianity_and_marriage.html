Christianity and Marriage(especially during these times)

Genesis 2:18 says, “Then the Lord God said, “It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him a helper fit for him.”

 

Found in the first book of the Old Testament, we are reminded of how beautiful marriage is. The opportunity to share life with someone is a beautiful thing, but with it can come many challenges. The changing world, the current pandemic, and the stressors that each of those creates can cause anxieties to increase.  What are some productive ways to maintain a healthy marriage with a Christian focus even during these trying times?

 

  1. Stop emphasizing productivity - focus instead on faithfulness.  Times are hard right now.  This is history in the making; routines are changed which can result in working from home, trying to homeschool children who want to see their friends, and an abundance of time that can be hard to fill.  Rather than trying to measure the success of your day based on how much you accomplished, look at how you and your spouse are getting through this time together.  Galatians 5:22 states:  “The fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control.”  Withhold judgment from your spouse and instead choose to celebrate these traits that your marriage reflects.

 

  1. Put your trust in God.  There are so many misleading things in the news today or filtering through social media.  Uncertainty between spouses on how to protect yourselves and your family can cause tension.  Make wise choices, but the best choice of all is to put your trust in God.  Stay focused on Christ and His wisdom.  Set aside daily time to seek His word together and pray for guidance and wisdom during this trying time.  ‘For the foolishness of God is wiser than human wisdom, and the weakness of God is stronger than human strength” (1 Corinthians 1:25). 

 

  1. Be humble.  Spending more and more time with someone in close proximity can minimize healthy separation, but we can seek to maintain humbleness.  When working from home or trying to help kids learn some new content, frustration may emerge.  Selfishness may temporarily reveal itself.  During these times when frustrations cause ugliness to shine, practice repentance and humility.  Work together to identify the problem and look for compromising solutions. 

 

  1. Encourage each other.  Spouses are a team.  Make it a point to encourage each other with a kind note, a small gift, or completing chores around the house that often go undone.  Share Bible verses that uplift and encourage your spouse.  The ways to encourage each other are endless, but the importance of such gestures is priceless. 

 

Throughout this pandemic and into the future, you and your spouse are a team.  You face a common enemy.  Work together to remind each other of what is true, worthy of praise, and commendable.  Seek to build a life founded on Scripture, celebrated through worship, empowered through prayer, and uplifted by each other’s encouragement.
