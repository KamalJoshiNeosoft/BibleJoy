5 Tips for Working Remotely

 

Colossians 3:23 says, ¨Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men….¨ This verse may be even more applicable in today's world given that work looks so different.  Many people are working from home. Let's take a closer look at some tips for working remotely.

 

  1.  Find a designated workspace.  Having a specific space to work in will help one stay mentally focused.  Look for a place in your home for an office.  This area should ideally offer some privacy.  Natural light will help brighten the area and improve mental health.  The designated workspace is also an efficient way to communicate with the people in charge.  Working remotely may require more frequent communication.  Also, stay connected with colleagues. Use online tools, teleconferences, or phone calls to communicate.  

 

  1. Strike a balance between comfort and efficiency.  Make sure the necessities are within reach.  It is difficult and stressful to complete one´s work without the proper equipment.  This may require some improvising, but make the workspace as comfortable as possible.  Place some family photos, a candle, or other comforting items in the space. 

 

  1. Set boundaries.  Setting boundaries may be the hardest part of working remotely.  With other people in the home, it can be hard to separate work and family.  Set some boundaries.  For example, if the door is closed ask others to respect the need for privacy.  Also, working remotely may be simpler in some ways, but it can also be very mentally and emotionally taxing.  The natural breaks that are present at work may not be as simple when working remotely.  Grabbing a cup of coffee in the workroom or going out for lunch are times when employees get rejuvenated.  Don't skip these breaks now.

 

  1. Maintain a schedule.  If there are children in the home, try to maintain a schedule.  Having a schedule for the children will help decrease distractions.  It can also be important to work while the kids are taking naps or having meals. 

 

  1. Consistency is key.  Set a work schedule.  Routine is not just important for the children.  Try to wake up at the same time each day and establish set work hours.  Start the day with the most difficult tasks.  This will help to complete the most mentally taxing work early in the day.
