5 Ways to Maintain a Positive Perspective

 

Maintaining a positive attitude can be hard. You have a flat tire. The drive thru gives you someone’s else’s order. You arrive ten minutes late to an important meeting. These negative occurrences are just some of the ways that our days can be turned into disarray, but when things like this (or much much worse) happen, we can keep our eyes on Christ. In doing this, we can stay focused with a positive perspective.

 

  1.  Perspective is everything.  There have been times in my life when I have just felt grumpy!  G-R-U-M-P-Y, and I quickly realized something.  The longer I was determined to feel sad, pouty, or just not cheerful, my day’s events followed that pattern.  Proverbs 15:13 states:  “A glad heart makes a cheerful face, but by sorrow of heart the spirit is crushed.”  Look up and know that you belong to the One who Created you.  That within itself is a powerful reminder as to why we need to focus intentionally on positivity.

 

  1. Things can always be worse.  When I think of Job, he went from having an abundance to having nothing.  Then he was blessed with double the bounty.  He could have allowed himself to become a miserable man.  Honestly, he could have still resented the things and people he lost even when he regained double, but he didn’t.  In reality, things can always be worse.  I understand that in the middle of tragedy or immense loss, it may not feel this way, but during those times push into God and His Word.

 

  1. What impact can your negativity have on others?  Proverbs 17:22 states:  “A joyful heart is good medicine, but a crushed spirit dries up the bones.”  When I am having a bad day, I can be quick to snap at my children, my colleagues, and even complete strangers.  But, when I do this, I am not presenting my Christlike values so others can see them.  Don’t get me wrong - we all have bad days, but when we choose to stay in the miry pit, we choose to diminish the blessings that we can provide to others. 

 

  1. Do something to help someone!  When life isn’t going as you would like, find a way to be a blessing to someone else.  Maybe this won’t fix your circumstances, but doing something nice for someone will certainly brighten their day.  I  have found that these random acts of kindness typically do as much (if not more) for me as they do for the other person.  It might not be easy to act this way when you are down and out, but give it a try!  Luke 6:27 reminds us: “But love your enemies, and do good, and lend, expecting nothing in return, and your reward will be great.”

 

  1. You are Redeemed!  If you are a child of God, no one can take that away from you.  On the worst of days, you are His!  On the best of days, you are His!  Nothing can distance you from His love.  There are no depths too far for Him to reach.  This alone is worthy of our praises and should empower us to reach for the positivity even when it seems hardest.  Galatians 2:20 states:  “I have been crucified with Christ. It is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me. And the life I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave himself for me.”
