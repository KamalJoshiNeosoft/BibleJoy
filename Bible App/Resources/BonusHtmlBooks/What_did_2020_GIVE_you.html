What did 2020 GIVE you?

 

From missed opportunities, limited gatherings, and other countless items, we can all reference multiple things lost to 2020, but have you considered what 2020 gave you? In a world where things are so different from the norm, it can be difficult to count our blessings, but, just the same, 2020 provided multiple things for us to celebrate. Psalms 118 reminds us: ¨Oh give thanks to the Lord, for he is good; for his steadfast love endures forever! Let Israel say, “His steadfast love endures forever.”

 

 

  1. More time in God's Word.  Simpler schedules brought more time to focus on God and His Word.  With no sports practices, no late night meetings, and no frantic schedules, people were able to find more time to study God's Word.  It is so easy to let our hectic lives distract us from His Word, but 2020 gave us an opportunity to slow down, create some better study habits, and be ready to take on 2021 armed with His Word in our hearts. 

 

  1. More family time.  2020 forced everyone to slow down.  We were afforded with more time to truly focus on our families.  Hectic schedules were replaced with loads of free time.  Free time can be difficult for many, but through the vacant planners came more meals around the dinner table, more home improvement projects done together, and more togetherness.  I am certainly thankful for the bonus family time that 2020 gave me. 

 

  1. The opportunity to try something new.  It is so easy to get stuck in a normal routine.  2020 redefined normal, and that redefining brought with it some new opportunities.  For example, one thing my family and I learned to love were hikes.  Rather than being stuck indoors, we took to the forests and found some beautiful natural scenery right here near our home.  It is highly likely that we would have never had this special time together if 2020 had not forced us to find something new to do.  DId I mention that those hiking moments will be special memories that we will cherish for the rest of our lives? 

 

  1. An emphasis on what really matters.  It is so easy to forget what truly matters.  As cliche as that may sound, we get caught up in the hustle and bustle of life and sometimes forget that the things that are filling our time won't truly matter in a few days, weeks, months, or even years. There are many things I have just taken for granted that I now count as opportunities rather than obligations.  There were many things that I had lost focus of that I regained thanks to 2020.  Mark 11:24 states:  ¨Therefore I tell you, whatever you ask in prayer, believe that you have received it, and it will be yours.¨  I believe that through my prayers, God has allowed me to see what really matters and focus more on that. 

 

  1. More prayer time.  Prayer time is always important.  2020 allowed us to have more time to pray without ceasing.  Rather than rushing out the door in the mornings, there was more time to slowly sip that cup of coffee while praying for our country, our homes, and our friends.  James 5:16 tells us:  ¨Therefore, confess your sins to one another and pray for one another, that you may be healed. The prayer of a righteous person has great power as it is working.¨

 

Sure, we can all list some things or sadly even someone we lost in 2020.  The adverse impacts of 2020 were overwhelming, but how great is it to know that even amidst such tragedy, we serve the Almighty King who holds the whole world in His Hands.  As we go through 2021, may we continue to make time for what is truly important.
