6 Psalms that Teach Us About Forgiveness

 

Forgiveness is no easy task, but the benefits of it are very favorable.  When we choose to forgive someone, we are not just just giving them a ‘get off the hook’ card so to speak. Instead, we are giving ourselves permission to move on. There have likely been people in your life who have hurt you and never asked for forgiveness. Those examples illustrate why we must move on and choose to forgive anyway. When we choose to forgive someone, we are freeing ourselves from the bondage that unforgiveness has on us. The decision to readmit someone to our lives following forgiveness is a different decision; nonetheless, choosing to forgive is a means of addressing your inner pain.

 

            God wants us to forgive others, but He also wants us to seek forgiveness from our own sins and shortcomings.  This can be just as hard as forgiving someone else.  We must be willing to be very open and transparent with God. 

 

            In the following sections, I would like to share six Psalms that teach us about forgiveness.

 

  1. Psalm 32 - Confess your sin.  “Then I acknowledged my sin to you and did not cover up my iniquity. I said, ‘I will confess my transgressions to the Lord.’ And you forgave the guilt of my sin” - Psalm 32:5.  In this Psalm, wwe see the importance of confessing our sin and asking for forgiveness for those sins.  When we confess our sins, He is just to forgive us. 

 

  1. Psalm 40 - Thank Him for forgiveness.  In Psalm 40, verses 2 & 3 state: “He lifted me out of the slimy pit, out of the mud and mire; he set my feet on a rock and gave me a firm place to stand. He put a new song in my mouth, a hymn of praise to our God. Many will see and fear the Lord and put their trust in Him.” This psalm reminds us that forgiving others is an important part of what we are asked and expected to do as Christians.  Read Matthew 18 to gain even more insights into Christ’s teachings about forgiving others. In this specific Psalm, we catch a glimpse of David praising God for forgiveness.  When we choose to let go of unforgiveness, we are preventing it from becoming sin in our own lives. 

 

  1. Psalm 103 - Unconditional love and forgiveness.  “As far as the east is from the west, so far as he removed our transgression from us.”  There is no sin too big for God.  He is able to clean up the vilest sinner and transform them into a new person living a clean life focused on God.  This Psalm is a poetic celebration of God’s love for us.  He is a compassionate Father who loves us unconditionally.  We have complete forgiveness because of the good, good Father He is. 

 

  1. Psalm 25 - No sin is too big.  “For the sake of your name, Lord, forgive my iniquity, though it is great.”  There are no sins too big or too bad to bring before the cross.  This verse from the twenty-fifth Psalm conveys David’s praise towards God and then His willingness to ask forgiveness from His sins.  Readers of this Psalm will see a very open David confessing his wickedness.  Being willing to confess our sins before God is a sign that we want forgiveness and are actively seeking it.

 

  1. Psalm 51 - Take Ownership for your Sin.  In Psalm 51 it states: “You do not delight in sacrifice, or I would bring it; you do not take pleasure in burnt offerings. My sacrifice, O God, is a broken spirit; a broken and contrite heart you, God, will not despise.” God had to acknowledge some major guilt following his affair with Bathsheba and the subsequent murdering of her husband/his friend, Uriah.  When confronted about his sin, David was honest and willing to confess it before God.  This example shows how we must take ownership of our sin. 

 

Psalm 130:3-4 states: “If you, Lord, kept a record of sins, Lord, who could stand?”  Thankfully, friend, He does not keep a record.  He forgives us and for that we should give reverence and serve Him! 

 

May these Psalms give you direction and purpose on your own path to seeking forgiveness.
