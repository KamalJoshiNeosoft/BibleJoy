10 Ways to Start Your Day with the Lord

 

Luke 1:78 reads, “Because the tender mercy of our God, whereby the sunrise shall visit us from on high.”

 

This verse reminds us each day is a gift from God.  Beginning each day, follow these 10 days tips to start your day with a focus on God.

 

  1. Thank God for waking you up.  This may seem simple, but it is a powerful reminder that this day is a gift from God. 

 

  1. Offer a prayer of thanksgiving.  “Then you will call on me and come and pray to me, and I will listen to you” (Jeremiah 29:12). 

 

  1. Find a way to make studying God’s Word part of your morning routine.  Listen to a podcast, read a morning devotion, or commit to a scripture study.  Feeding your soul with Words from the Holy Bible will help sustain you through the day.

 

  1. Take notice of God’s beauty.  Whether you live in a remote area or an urban city, God’s handiwork is all around us.  Take notice of that beautiful sunrise or that colorful flower adorning the sidewalk.  There is so much God has made for us to appreciate!

 

  1. Help other people.  Helping others is an extension of being God’s hands and feet. 

 

  1. Find a way to lighten someone else’s load.  Look around to find someone who needs help.  Take a few minutes to help them. 

 

  1. Walk Before you Run.  Mornings can be hectic, but slow down.  Be still and allow God to energize you for the day.

 

  1. Remain Positive.  Even when the day is off to a bumpy start, remember that God’s joy is our strength.  Nehemiah 8:10 tells us to “Go and enjoy choice food and sweet drinks, and send some to those who have nothing prepared.  This day is holy to our Lord.  Do not grieve, for the joy of the Lord is your strength.” 

 

  1. Be open to change.  Change is hard.  When we look throughout the Bible though we see so many people who had to endure change.  From the Israelites to Jonah, strong Biblical characters had to be willing to follow God’s path rather than their own.

 

  1. Count your blessings.  Counting our blessings rather than our problems may sound cheesy, but it really is a great way to enhance our moods.  Alarm clocks can be annoying, but thinking about all the blessings that follow the alarm clock can be a powerful tool to transform our frowns into anticipation. 

 

Follow these ten tips to start your day with the Lord.  And, above all else, remember:  Psalm 118:24:  “This is the day the Lord has made;  We will rejoice and be glad in it.”
