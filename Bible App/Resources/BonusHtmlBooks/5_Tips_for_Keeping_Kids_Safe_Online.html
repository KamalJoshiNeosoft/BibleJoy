5 Tips for Keeping Kids Safe Online

 

Todayś children and youth spend so much time online. From school assignments to personal devices, the amount of time kids spend online is enough reason for concern.  Letś take a closer look at five tips for keeping kids safe online.

 

1.      Set expectations. Setting expectations for your child is important. They should know what you expect of them. If we discuss what is acceptable and unacceptable with our children, they are more likely to follow our safety advice. Make sure your child knows about keeping information private when online. It is also important to remind them of how important it is to have healthy relationships offline too.  All too often, children can get caught up in wanting to communicate via texting or other platforms.

 

2.      Use an app to monitor your child's online activities.  Trust is a powerful tool that certainly should not be dismissed, but in today's world, parents cannot be too safe.  Many i-Phones and Androids come with parental monitoring.  Screen time is a useful way to monitor online activities and even app downloads on the iPhone.  Android has an app called Family Link. 

 

3.      Be real.  Discuss the dangers of making poor choices online.  Allow your child to ask questions.  Be honest and open in helping them understand how powerful our online choices are.  Consider explaining to your child that our choices should always be honest, admirable, and not require hiding. 

 

4.      Teach them how to take action.  If your child should encounter something online or via a personal device that seems wrong or somehow odd, tell them to keep those messages, but don't respond.  They need to show those messages to you in a timely manner.  From there you can decide on the next steps. 

 

5.    Pray for them and with them.  The decision to allow a child to use a device seems very natural, but must be done so with caution and maturity.  Pray for your child and their future decisions.  As they age, they will have more independence.  Gently guide them as Christ would guide them. Pray with them that they will always make choices that reflect their Christian values.  James 5:16 reminds us:  ¨Therefore, confess your sins to one another and pray for one another, that you may be healed. The prayer of a righteous person has great power as it is working.¨

 

Proverbs 1:7 states:  ¨The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge; fools despise wisdom and instruction.¨   Use this verse  as a means of instructing your children about how God wants us to grow in wisdom and experiences, but we need to listen to instruction.
