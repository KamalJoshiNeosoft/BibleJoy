5 Ways to Enhance Your Prayer Life

 

1 Thessalonians 5:17 says, “Pray without ceasing.” As busy human beings, running from one task to the next, we tend to always want to put a period at the end of a task, a sentence, an event, and so on. But, this simple verse reminds us of something that is anything but simple. Let’s take a look at 5 ways to enhance your prayer life.

 

  1.  Forget the Amen. God wants us to talk to Him without the period or the Amen.  He wants us to remain in constant dialogue with Him about our hopes, our ups and downs, and all the seemingly simple things throughout our day. We are quick to text our friends about the neat bird we saw during our evening walk or the amazing dessert we tried with lunch, but we forget to tell God these simple things.  It may seem overdone, but if it is worth sharing with a friend, it is certainly worth sharing with the Ultimate Friend, Our Heavenly Father.  Thank Him for  His creation - that beautiful bird or the talent He gave humans, so they could make our taste buds erupt with glee.

 

  1. Don’t overcomplicate it.  At different times in my life, I have approached prayer in many different ways.  Do you know what I always come back to?  It is not meant to be a process that looks the same from one time to the next.  It is a conversation with my Heavenly Father that may take place while I am driving down the road.  It may be while I am walking down the hall at work.  Prayer isn’t meant to be complex or overwhelming.  It is meant to be a conversation where we submit and acknowledge that He is always with us.  His Word says in Ephesians 6:18:  “Praying at all times in the Spirit, with all prayer and supplication.  To that end keep alert with all perseverance, making supplication for all the saints.”

 

  1. Find some time for truly focused prayer.  While this seems opposite of the first two tips I provided, this particular prayer is about the times when you can communicate with God free from distraction.  You are able to share your praises and concerns, but you are also able to pause and allow Him to speak back to you.  In Jeremiah 33:3, His Word reads: “Call to me and I will answer you, and will tell you the great and hidden things that you have not known.”  

 

  1. Create a prayer wall.  Find a place in your home where you can post your prayers.  Have one place designated for answered prayers.  Have a different space to post prayers that you are waiting for an answer to. 

 

  1. Pay attention to all the answered prayers. Pay attention as these unanswered prayers take on new identities when you move them to the answered column.  When the answered prayer space becomes too crowded, consider placing these in a box or a notebook to look back on when you need a reminder of James 5:16:  “The prayer of a righteous person has great power as it is working.”   

 

These tips are meant to help you grow spiritually.  Yes, God does want us to enter into our prayer closets and communicate with Him using the most intimate parts of ourselves, but we should also remember that we can pray without ceasing all throughout our day!
