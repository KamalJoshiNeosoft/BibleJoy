8 Life Tips from the Four Gospels

 

The Four Gospels are filled with many life lessons. Far more lessons than can be sufficiently covered here. But, within these four books, there are many core character traits that can be observed and applied to our daily lives.

 

  1. Dishonesty does not have to define you.  There are many times that people say, ¨Well, you know what he did way back then….¨ Matthew is an excellent example of what it means to turn one's life around.  Initially, Matthew was a sketchy Jewish tax-collector.  He gave up his dishonest ways and became Christ's disciple.  Throughout his book, readers learn a lot about money.  Matthew turned from his dishonest ways and wanted everyone to know that Jesus is the Messiah. 

 

  1. Stay strong!  Mark wrote one of the four gospels.  He wanted his love and adoration for Christ to be the focal point for his writing.  Mark recorded Jesus´ life and ministry.  Mark remained faithful even when things were tough.  Mark 11:23:  ¨Truly, I say to you, whoever says to this mountain, ‘Be taken up and thrown into the sea,’ and does not doubt in his heart, but believes that what he says will come to pass, it will be done for him.¨

 

  1. Be healed.  Luke wanted everyone to be a part of Christ's family and be healed of their sins.  As a physician, Luke wrote about the life and ministry of Jesus for non-Jewish readers.  He wanted everyone to know that this was the Messiah who had been promised.  Throughout these chapters, one´s heart can be changed.

 

  1. Be unique and be bold.  John takes a unique perspective by showing us a view of Jesus from Heaven down.  There were many signs and symbols that illustrate how Jesus is the Messiah.  Mark 11:23:  ¨Truly, I say to you, whoever says to this mountain, ‘Be taken up and thrown into the sea,’ and does not doubt in his heart, but believes that what he says will come to pass, it will be done for him.¨

 

  1. Be compassionate.  In John 11, Jesus showed compassion at Lazarus´ grave.  He saw Lazarus´ friends weeping, and He wept alongside them.  Throughout the gospels, Jesus Christ felt compassion towards people, both comforting and healing them.

 

  1. Sometimes there is no explanation.  In all four gospels, the feeding of the 5,000 appears.  This miracle is found in Matthew 14; Mark 6; Luke 9; and John 6.  When it cannot be explained, just smile and say ¨thank you, Jesus¨.  It did not make sense how Jesus was able to take two tiny fish and two loaves of bread and feed thousands of people.  There are miracles from Jesus that occur in humans´ lives daily. 

 

  1. Have faith.  Throughout the four gospels, readers witness miracles over and over again.  We walk by faith not by sight.  In doing so, we express our faith and allow it to take us to new heights.  From the woman who simply wanted to touch Jesus’ garment to the little boy with his fishes, having faith shows that we trust Jesus to direct our lives and take care of us. 

 

  1. Be teachable.  In reading the parables throughout the Four Gospels, readers can see amazing stories about how we should plant His Word in good soil and remain tillable.  When we become harsh and critical, our seeds can fall into spaces they cannot grow new Christians for His Kingdom.  Never lose a willingness to learn from life.
