Finding the Positive in the Storm

 

Choosing to see the silver lining in the storm can be hard. There are times we feel so overwhelmed, so tired, and so frustrated that it can be hard to not groan and let the tears flow. What does the Bible say about finding the positive in the storm?

 

Psalm 46:1-3 says: “God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble. Therefore we will not fear, though the earth give way and the mountains fall into the heart of the sea, though its waters roar and foam and the mountains quake with their surging.”

If we break this verse down, we can see several promises from God.

  1. He is our refuge.  His arms are a safe place.  When life is hard, rest in Him.  Don’t try to fix things on your own.  Seek Him.
  2. He is our strength.  When our strength fails, His remains strong enough to hold us and keep us going. 
  3. He is always present.  Do you see those words ‘ever-present’?  That means He is always with us.  He is the friend that sticks closer than a brother.
  4. Do not fear.  This verse tells us that we should not fear.  Remain focused on Him and know that a lot of bad things may happen, but God’s love and presence are unchanging.
  5. The world is uncertain, but He is very certain.  This verse mentions the mountains falling, the waters roaring, but God remains.  Take comfort in knowing that He is near. 

There are several more verses that remind us of God’s promises and help during the stormiest seasons in life.  Consider writing these verses on index cards and posting in different places as visible reminds of His love and guidance. 

Proverbs 18:10:  “The name of the Lord is a strong tower; the righteous run into it and are safe.”

Nehemiah 8:10:  “Do not grieve, for the joy of the Lord is your strength.”

Isaiah 41:10: “So do not fear, for I am with you; do not be dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you and help you; I will uphold you with my righteous right hand.”

Exodus 15:2:  “The Lord is my strength and my song; he has given me victory. This is my God, and I will praise him—  my father’s God, and I will exalt him!”

            Although it can be hard when we are in the middle of the storm, in much the same way, God gave Joshua strength and wisdom when he was leading the Israelites, He will guide you.  In the same way, He sent manna from Heaven, He will provide for you.  He will guide you in the same way, He was there for Barnabas when he went to Tarsus.  And, when He walks you through the storm, remember to encourage one another and build one another up as noted in 1 Thessalonians 5:11.

It may be hard to see, but better days are coming!  Seek Him and find the positive in the storm.
