How to Do God's Work, When Stuck at Home

 

Doing God’s work from home may seem difficult, but there are options. Consider these four different ways to reach out from the comfort of your home and potentially make a big impact on your friends, neighbors, and community.

 

Social media has become a very effective communication tool. If you use Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, or another form of social media, harness the opportunity to spread positive messages. The world is looking for hope and all too often social media is filled with grim news. Posts do not have to be lengthy or detailed, but share a favorite Bible verse.  Add a story about how God pulled you through a difficult time or how you received unexpected blessings.  These posts may become the highlight of someone’s day!  Psalm 96:3 says:  “Declare his glory among the nations, his marvelous works among all the peoples!”

 

Be kind! Building relationships even with people from afar can have a huge impact.  When walking outside or doing yard work, extend a smile and a wave when people pass.  While you may not be having direct contact with the people who pass, you are showing Christ’s love by extending warmth to those who see you.  This may seem simple, but it can be a powerful way to do God’s work from home.  Galatians 6:10:  “So then, as we have opportunity, let us do good to everyone, and especially to those who are of the household of faith.”

 

Offer to lend a helping hand.  Similar to being kind, offering to lend a helping hand to neighbors is another effective way to do God’s work.  Raking leaves, mowing the lawn, helping a neighbor who is completing some minor repairs….all of these tasks present opportunities to build relationships and be a witness for God.  The conversations held during these times may be brief or not about Biblical topics at all, but they are still important in treating others the way we want to be treated.  Matthew 5:16:   “In the same way, let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven.”

 

Use the kitchen to serve others.  The kitchen can be a powerful tool.  Baked items, simple casseroles, or other meal ideas can be created and passed out to people in need.  Use some basic ingredients, add some love, and find people who can benefit from an outpouring of love.  This simple gesture can be a concrete example of using simple means to spread a much bigger message.  Proverbs 19:17:  “Whoever is generous to the poor lends to the Lord, and he will repay him for his deed. – Proverbs 19:17”

 

            Each of these actions may seem simple...they may seem unable to create change, but each one can be linked to God’s instruction. Start today - do God’s work from home!
