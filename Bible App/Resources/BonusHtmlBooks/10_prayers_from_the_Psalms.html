10 prayers from the Psalms

 

Throughout the book of Psalms, readers experience firsthand the sacred songs and poems many of which are from some of David’s most sentimental and heartfelt moments.  The following prayers taken from Psalms provide guidance and specific prayers for 7 different situations.

 

  1. To give praise.  In Psalm 30, David is dedicating his palace, but these beautifully written words become a symbol of praise.  Throughout this Psalm, he invites the congregation to join him in praise.  Conder praying this prayer of praise now.

 

Dear Father, we sing praises to you o Lord….as your saints, we sing praises and give thanks to your Holy Name.  Lord, we know that your anger is for but a moment, but your favor is for a lifetime.  Lord, we know that weeping may tarry for the night, but joy comes in the morning.  Thank you for, Lord, for your many wondrous words.  We love you.  Amen. 

 

 

  1. To trust in God.  At times when evil seems to be drowning our days in sorrow, Psalm 37 can be a powerful reminder of God’s faithfulness.  We should trust in Him to bring justice. 

 

Dear Heavenly Father, I put my trust in you, Lord.  I know that you tell us to dwell in the land and befriend faithfulness.  Lord, I choose to delight and trust in you, Lord.  I know that you say that you will give me the desires of my heart.  I ask now for you to humbly help me to let you work and to remove my fears and struggles from my view.  I want to put my trust in you.  This I ask in your name, Father.  Amen. 

 

 

  1. To pray for deliverance.  Do you need deliverance?  Are you walking in despair?  Turn to Psalm 40 for guidance about how to ask God to help you see the future.

 

 

My precious Lord, I come before you now and I know that you are waiting patiently for me to confess this despair to you.  I know that you hear me when I cry.  I am asking you to lift me out of the pit of destruction.  Father, pull me from the miry bog and set my feet upon a rock.  Father, I need you to make my steps secure.  This I ask and pray in your Holy name.  Amen. 

 

 

  1. To pray for protection.  Even when our fear feels like too much, Psalms 46 reminds us that God has declared our protection.  God is in control.  Pray this prayer to ask Him to calm us and allow us to be still while He works. 

 

My sweet Lord and Savior, I need your protection.  I want to be still and exalt you as the one true living God.  You deserve to be exalted above the nations.  I want you to be exalted in all of the earth.  You are the Lord of hosts, and I declare now that you are with me.  I know that you are my fortress and my protection.  Thank you in advance for all you are doing in my life.  Amen. 

 

 

 

  1. Are you feeling overwhelmed?  Psalm 55 reminds us that we are not lost at sea.  We are not thrown about with no hope. God provides hope and protection.  Pray this prayer with me now to surrender your feelings of being overwhelmed to Him. 

 

Dear Lord, I am feeling so overwhelmed.  My burdens are heavy, but I know that you give strength and protection.  I come to you now casting my burdens on you, Lord.  I know you will sustain me.  I know that you will never permit me as one of your righteous children to be moved.  Lord, I thank you for that security, that affirming love.  Lord, my anxieties are great, but I want to leave those at the foot of the cross.  Even now, Lord, I declare that you are Lord and you are guiding me.  I love you, Lord.  Amen. 

 

 

  1. To give thanks for His mercy.  God’s covenant, love, and mercy is undeniable.  In Psalm 106, it tells the story about how God hears us even when we make poor choices. How great it is that we can declare thanksgiving in knowing that His mercies are new every morning. 

 

Lord, I come before you now praising you for your mercies that are new every morning.  Lord you are a good, good Father.  I know that your steadfast love endures forever, and I am so very blessed to be a recipient of your mercy.  Lord, may I take refuge in your mercies and always lift up gratitude to you.  Thank you, Father.  Amen. 

 

 

  1. To ask for forgiveness.  David comes before the Lord in Psalm 51 to acknowledge his guilt and shame following his affair with Bathsheba and the murdering of her husband.  This prayer guides us to confess our sins before the Lord and know that He is just to forgive us. 

 

 

Dear Heavenly Father, I am bringing before you the declaration of my sin.  The acknowledgement that I have sinned and cannot correct is on my own.  Father, you tell us there is no need for burnt offerings, but you desire we come before you with a broken spirit.  My heart and spirit are broken.  They are contrite.  I know that you despise these things, and I want to ask your forgiveness.  I have sinned, and I need your help.  Please forgive me, Lord.  Amen. 

  1.  To get help during distress:  In Psalm 61, David is crying out in distress to God.  He is far from home.  This prayer directs us to confess our needs before God. 

 

Dear Heavenly Father:  Hear my cry, O God.  Please help me and listen to my prayer.  From the ends of the earth, I call to you.  My heart feels faint.  Please Lord lead me to the rock that is higher than I.  I need you dear Lord.  This I pray in Jesus name.  Amen.

 

  1. To get help when feeling lost.  In Psalm 63, David is crying out to God from the wilderness.  You can pray this prayer to ask God to meet you where you are today. 

 

You, God, are my God, I seek you.  My heart thirsts for you.  My whole being is longing for you.  I am in a dry and poached land where there is no water.  I have been in your sanctuary and I know your power and your glory.  I know that your love is better than my life.  I glorify you, Lord.  I am praising you now and will continue to do so for as long as I live.  I lift my hands to you today, Father.  I praise you.  You are my help, Father.  I cling to you today.  Father, please direct me and give me peace and calmness from feeling so lost.  This I ask in your name.  Amen.

 

 

  1.  To acknowledge being created in His image.  This prayer shows David's acknowledgement of God's presence at conception.  He is admiring God's willingness to make us in His own image with uniqueness in His design. 

 

Dear God, you have searched me.  Lord, you know me.  You know when I sit and when I rise.  You perceive my thoughts from a distance.  You know when I go and when I lay down. You are familiar with my ways.  Lord, no knowledge is too great for you.  You are with me, and you saw fit to create me in your image and your likeness.  Thank you, Father.  I do pray and celebrate your design today.  I praise you today, Lord, and ask this in your name.  Amen.
