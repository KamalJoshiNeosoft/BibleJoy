How to Stay Connected to God in a Hectic World

 

For weeks and in some cases, months, our daily lives seem to come to an abrupt halt. Now that life is slowly resuming to its regular pace, you may be wondering how you can stay connected to God. Here are four tips to help you see and feel God even in the most rushed circumstances.

 

  1.  Don't overlook nature.  One of the ways that I always feel the closest to God is by looking at the things He has created.  Trees, flowers, streams, mountains, and the list goes on.  I always feel like I am looking at God when I truly take time to admire these simple treasures.  In Chapters 1 and 2 of Genesis, we are presented with the story of God's work to create these treasures.  I truly believe that when we are willing to pause and reflect on the things surrounding us that are not man made, we get a firsthand glimpse of God.  At that time, we may feel compelled to pray or have conversation with God.

 

  1.  Start the day with God before you truly start the day.  Even if you are not a morning person, waking up a little early to listen to a podcast, study from the Bible, or meditate on your daily devotion, creating a routine that begins with God is invaluable in allowing Him to frame your day.  By taking time to study God's word at the beginning of the day, you are declaring it to be the most important aspect of your life.  As this process continues, it becomes more and more obvious why He deserves those initial moments.  Hebrews 4:12 states, ¨For the word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soup and spirit, of joints and marrow, and discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart¨.  Make your intention to start your day with Him!

 

  1.  In Exodus 33:14, the Bible says, ¨And he said, ¨My presence will go with you, and I will give you rest.¨  This verse means that He is always with us - there is no reason that you cannot talk with God during the most hectic or stressful times of your day.  He is with you in the stressful work meeting.  He is with you during the lonely part of the day when you leave your children at school or daycare.  His presence is forever, and this allows us to know that our relationship with Him is natural. 

 

  1.  Don't try to force it.  As humans, sometimes we try too hard, but God does not want us to feel pressured or even more rushed because we are trying to take more time for Him.  In fact, He wants our relationship with Him to be natural.  One of the best ways to know He is with us during a hectic day is simply to acknowledge the genuine nature of His love.  Proverbs 18:24 reminds us, ¨A man that hath friends must shew himself friendly: and there is a friend that sticketh closer than a brother¨.  This friend does not get upset when we fail to say good morning or we forget their child's soccer game.  God is that friend who stands closer than a brother.  Allow Him to be your calm in the hectic world.
