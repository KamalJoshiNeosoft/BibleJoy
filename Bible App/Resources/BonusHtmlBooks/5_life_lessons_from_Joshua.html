5 Life Lessons from the Book of Joshua

 

What lessons can we learn from the sixth book in the Old Testament? The book of Joshua takes readers into Israel’s story following the conquest of Canaan. Let’s take a look at five key lessons that are evidenced throughout this text.

 

  1.  Evil does exist, but God’s power is sufficient to defeat it. In Joshua 1:5-8, readers meet Joshua who has become the Israelites’ new leader. Joshua had every right to be afraid or discouraged with so many people there. He did not allow the present evil to fill him with weakness.  Instead, Joshua focused on what the Lord had promised him.  Through Joshua’s belief in God’s power, he was armed with strength and knowledge to defeat the enemy.  What enemies do you need to surrender today, so that you can take full advantage of God’s presence and commitment in your life. 

 

  1. With God, there is always a way.  There are times in life that people we truly believed cared about abandon us or let us down, but God will never be one of these individuals.  He is with us through every circumstance and trial.  In Joshua 3:9-17, we see firsthand how God was able to work within the land of Canaan.  Through obedience, the Israelites watched the Jordan River split, so they could cross.  The Israelites would have thought there was no way, but God made a way.  He will make a way for us when it seems that there is no way.

 

  1. Practice Patience.  In Joshua 6:1-5, the Israelites are walking around the city of Jericho.  Their minds are focused on going to battle.  They feel this is needed, but Joshua told them to be quiet.  It was through his obedience to follow the Lord’s instructions, the Israelites kept marching.  After marching around the city for six days and then marching seven times on the seventh day, they blew their trumpets and the walls came tumbling down.  How many times do we not know God’s full plan, so we are non-compliant?  How differently would things have been if Joshua hadn’t heeded the Lord’s instructions? 

 

  1. Stay Focused.  Throughout the book of Joshua, we see time and time again the need for perseverance.  Remaining focused was necessary.  Keeping a focus on God and His instructions was necessary.  We must stay focused and not lose heart when things get hard. 

 

  1. Don’t Accept Defeat!  Rather than choosing to run, Joshua chose over and over again to stay.  He did not allow fear or weakness to lead him away from God’s battle plan.  Stay focused on God’s plan - don’t accept defeat.
