6 ways to use technology to share your testimony

 

Today we use technology for so many different tasks. From ordering food and having it delivered to budgeting our chances, technology creates organization and accessibility. Have you ever considered how technology can be used to share your testimony?

 

  1. Social media.  Many people have social media accounts.  These accounts are one small way to make a big impact.  My 73-year-old mom uses Facebook.  While keyboarding, formatting, and using the social media giant isn’t her area of expertise, she types out a Bible verse and posts it every single day.  Simple? Yes!  Powerful? Yes! 

 

  1. Email friends and acquaintances.  If you have a special burden for someone, use email to reach out to them.  Putting your words into writing can make a different impact than a phone call or informal text.  1 John 1:7 says, ¨But if we walk in the light, as he is in the light, we have fellowship with one another, and the blood of Jesus his Son cleanses us from all sin.¨

 

  1. Don’t be afraid of phone calls.  While many people don’t take the time to make phone calls like they once did, this technology still remains a powerful tool for connectivity.  Get involved through your local church and find out how you can make contact with guests who have visited.  Following up with these contacts can make a huge impact on their interest in the church and indirectly with God.

 

  1. Tune in to Christian stations.  If you find yourself in a car rental, return the car with the dial tuned in to your favorite Christian station.  The next person to drive the car may hear a message that oils ultimately save their life and their soul. Hebrews 4:12 shows us how powerful hearing the word can be:  ¨For the word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soul and of spirit, of joints and of marrow, and discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart.¨

 

  1. YouTube can be for everyone. Do you feel like God has given you a clear message? Does He want you to speak out more? Do you have talents that can be utilized via a YouTube channel?  Seek God’s will!  If He brings you to it, He will bring you through it.

 

  1. Stay connected.  A simple text message, a comment on someone’s social media post, or a post that you make on social media are all ways to create connectivity.  In a world where people should be more connected than ever before, some people feel very alienated, isolated, and seeking human companionship.  Allow the technology you have at your fingertips to be the springboard for even greater levels of connectivity. 

 

Technology results in a lot of wasted time for many people; nonetheless, it also gives us the opportunity to make connections and lasting impacts on people we would not have otherwise been able to reach.  Romans 12:6 states:  ¨Having gifts that differ according to the grace given to us, let us use them: if prophecy, in proportion to our faith¨.  Look for ways to use technology to share your testimony.
