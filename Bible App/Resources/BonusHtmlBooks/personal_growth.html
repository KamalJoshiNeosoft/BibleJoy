Five Characteristics of Personal Growth

 

Personal growth is about taking steps to develop your mind, your health, and your spirituality. Let’s take a look at five components of personal growth.

 

  1. Find the seed. What inspires you? What appears to be your destiny? God created you because He needed someone exactly like you on this earth. Pray for direction in how to channel the seed of your destiny from within. Strive to seek God’s direction and allow Him to lead you down different paths to grow, develop, and refine that seed. Jeremiah 29:11: “For I know the plans I have for you,” declares the Lord, “plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future.”

 

  1. Take responsibility.  God wants us to take responsibility.  Part of responsibility means that we have to be able and willing to accept the free will He has given us and still make healthy choices.  Personal growth is reflected through the choice to do the right thing.  Act and respond in a way that reflects Christian wisdom and leads to others’ benefit.  Personal growth is shown in individuals who are able to give more and take less.   Ezra 10:4: “Arise, for it is your task, and we are with you; be strong and do it.”

 

  1. Give to others.  The way we pour into other people’s lives is a reflection of how we support and give to others.  Now, the five languages could easily be applied here.  There are multiple ways to give to people.  Physical gifts, kind words, acts of service, a warm smile or a tender hug, and quality time all represent products of personal growth.  Find ways to give more than you take and you will see your own self-worth and fulfillment growing within your Christian walk.  2 Corinthians 9:6-8:  “Remember this: Whoever sows sparingly will also reap sparingly, and whoever sows generously will also reap generously. Each of you should give what you have decided in your heart to give, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver.”

 

  1. Be positive.  Create a positive mindset.  This is reflective of personal growth and creates a metaphorical basket to hold God’s blessings.  Counting one’s blessings and walking a life of optimism is a sign of personal growth.  Psalm 34:8: “Taste and see that the Lord is good; blessed is the one who takes refuge in him.”

 

  1. Be a faith builder.  When we have a favorable outlook on life and share that perspective with other people, we are showing other people that we believe in the power of positivity.   Galatians 6:2:  “ Carry each other’s burdens, and in this way you will fulfill the law of Christ.’

 

These five components may not reflect typical personal growth, but each of these suggestions reflects confidence in Christ and a desire to be a stronger, more faith driven individual.
