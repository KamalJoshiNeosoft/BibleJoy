5 prayer tips inspired by Psalms

 

Philippians 4:6 states: ¨Do not be anxious about anything, but in everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known to God.¨

 

The book of Psalms is a beautifully written book in the Old Testament.  Throughout its pages, readers find powerful praises echoed from the thoughts of David and other Old Testament writers.  Looking at these chapters can be transformational for our prayer lives  Let's take a closer look at 5 prayers tips inspired by Psalms:

 

  1. New ways of expressing our prayers.  It is easy to allow prayer to become repetitious.  Praying through the Psalms changes that.  Rather than yesterday´s before school prayer with our children becoming today´s before school prayer, the Psalms gives us a fresh perspective.  It prompts us to pray about things we would not normally pray about.  There are many situations that we pray about continually because they are important to us.  Praying through the Psalms will give us new ways of expressing our thoughts.  For example, instead of saying:  ¨Lord, please be with my family¨, Psalm 3:3 leads us to pray:  ¨Lord, please be a shield around my family today.¨

 

  1. More Biblically faithful prayers.  Praying through the Psalms provides words that God gave us.  We are able to pray these words back to Him in a powerful gesture of submission, praise, and thanksgiving.  By praying His Words, we are praying more in accordance with HIs Word and His will for our lives. 

 

  1. More God-centered prayers.  Praying through the Psalms provides a more God-focused prayer.  As humans, we are selfish.  Our prayers can become selfish too.  When we pray through the Psalms, we are able to focus more on the will and attributes of God.  This Biblically centered prayer removes some of the selfishness from us.  We are more attentive to God and His Kingdom when we use the Psalms as a central focus for our prayer time. 

 

  1. More Focused Prayers.  The many hectic things our days consist of prompts our prayers to sometimes be a jumbled mess of randomness.  When we pray the Psalms, our prayers can be more focused.  Rather than losing focus or praying through repetitiveness, praying through the Psalms can become a more disciplined time for you to truly communicate with the Creator of the Universe, the Savior of the World.  If during such prayer time, your thoughts become unfocused, look at the next verse and focus back in on what the Holy Bible says.

 

  1. Real Conversations with the One Real God.  Rather than praying in monotone, praying through the Psalms can be a powerful way to have real conversation with God.  Praying the Psalms back to Him can be a powerful way to pause, reflect, and allow Him to speak back to us.
