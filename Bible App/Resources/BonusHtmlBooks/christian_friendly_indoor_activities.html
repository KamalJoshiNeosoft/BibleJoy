Christian Friendly Indoor Activities

 

Finding activities to do indoors that keep everyone busy can be hard! Families are spending more time at home than ever before. Romans 12:12 reminds us “Rejoice in hope, be patient in tribulation, be constant in prayer.” While the current reality may be different from several months ago, the opportunity to slow down presents a less rushed pace and opportunity to try some new activities. Here are some of our favorite activities for a day indoors!

 

  1. Play trivia.  Use your imagination to stage a gameshow.  Consider finding a pre-made Bible trivia game at jeopardylabs.com or kahoot.com.  These interactive platforms make it easy to bring trivia questions into the home with 21st century style.

 

  1. Go through old photos.  While most of us take pictures digitally today, going back and looking at photos from a few years (or even a few months) ago can provide some joyful reminiscing.  Psalm 16:11 - “You make known to me the path of life; in your presence there is fullness of joy; at your right hand are pleasures forevermore” shows us how the pause to look back at previous events can bring celebration and fullness!

 

  1. Looking for some new TV viewing options?  Check out PureFlix.com for a variety of faith and family-friendly options.  There is even a Summer Adventures category that will prove of high interest to kids of all ages. 

 

  1. Set up a science lab!  Kids and adults alike love science.  Check out https://www.businessinsider.com/8-awesomely-simple-science-experiments-you-can-do-at-home-2016-7 for some simple science experiments that will take you back to those fun lab days at school.  Who says you can’t still enjoy the satisfaction of creating a tornado in a bottle?

 

  1. Grab some blankets, some popcorn, and the family!  Create a fort and create a unique movie night.  Use lights to mimic the night sky and watch a movie in your fort.

 

  1.  Have a game night.  Bring out your favorite board game or cards for some friendly competition. 

 

  1. Use some of the extra time to gain an even greater awareness of your favorite book in the Bible or even your favorite verse.  God’s Word teaches us in Psalm 119:11 -  “I have stored up your word in my heart, that I might not sin against you.” 

 

 

Being indoors will allow some added opportunities to get creative!  Use these ideas or think of your own, but most importantly, remember Romans 8:28:  “And we know that for those who love God all things work together for good, for those who are called according to his purpose.”
