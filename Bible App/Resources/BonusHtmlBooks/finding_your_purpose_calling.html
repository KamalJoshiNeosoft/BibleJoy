Finding Your Purpose/Calling

 

Have you ever considered how God works all things together - even your life - according to His purposes. He has numbered our days and will fulfill every purpose He has for you. “I cry out to God Most High, to God who fulfills his purpose for me” Psalm 57:2. We make choices and decisions that impact our lives and how those days He has planned for us play out. When seeking the purpose/ calling for your life, there are some questions to ask yourself. An answer to any of these questions may reveal roadblocks to finding/fulfilling your purpose.

 

  1.  Are you living in unforgiven sin?  If so, confess your sin to God.  As we already noted, we must make choices that reflect Christian wisdom and traits.

 

  1. Are you lacking joy and excitement?  If so, this may be a stronger reason for you to apply the following suggestions more assertively as you seek your purpose/calling. 

 

  1. Do you lack fulfillment?  If so, do not let this moment keep you blocked from your true purpose.

 

  1. Do you feel stuck?  If so, ask God for direction.  His path is sure, and He will make a way. 

 

After thinking about these four questions, look at the five following steps towards finding your purpose or calling for we know that Romans 8:28 says: “And we know that for those who love God all things work together for good, for those who are called according to his purpose.”

 

 

  1.  Go to God in prayer.  James 1:5 states, "If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask God, who gives generously to all without reproach, and it will be given him."  If you are unsure of what happens next - talk to God.

 

  1. Dig into God’s Word.  One of the primary ways God communicates with us is through HIs Word.  Psalm 119:105 says, "Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path."  Allow His Word to illuminate your path and help you find your purpose/calling.

 

  1. Assess your talents.  What are your gifts and talents?  How can you use your unique gifts and skills to help other people.  Being able to lock in somewhere that we can help other people is a meaningful way to find one’s purpose.

 

  1. Ask other people for help.  Proverbs 11:14 says, "Where there is no guidance, a people falls, but in an abundance of counselors there is safety."  If you are having trouble figuring out what seems best, seek the help of someone whom you trust that can give you direction and help you pray for direction. 

 

  1. Trust God.    You may feel confused, but God isn’t confused.  Pray and wait on His timing.
