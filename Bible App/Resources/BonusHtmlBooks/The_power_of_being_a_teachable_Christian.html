The Power of being a Teachable Christian

 

Depending on personality types and confidence levels, there are people who know plenty but still are unsure of themselves. On the other hand, sometimes people feel like their knowledge has reached a pinnacle of expertise. While this is not a bad mouth, the Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy to be ¨Always learning and never able to arrive at a knowledge of the truth¨. What are some ways we can remain a teachable Christian?

 

  1.  Proverbs 22:6 states:  ¨Train up a child in the way he should go; even when he is old he will not depart from it.¨  Regardless of whether you were raised in the church or came to accept Christ in your later years, there is a lot of learning and growing to do.  Christ made us in His image, but we will never be done learning and applying the lessons He gave us. 

 

  1. Matthew 18:4 states:  ¨Whoever humbles himself like this child is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven.¨  Careful consideration of this verse allows us to see that we should always be seeking God's direction.   The way a small child seeks directions from its parent, we should be seeking wisdom and understanding from God.  How many times does an adult child still reach out to an elder for advice?  How many times have you heard an adult say, ¨I wish I could ask my mom for advice just one more time….¨  This simple application allows us to see how God wants us to remain teachable. 

 

  1. Proverbs 12:15 states:  ¨The way of a fool is right in his own eyes, but a wise man listens to advice.¨  Emotions can override intellect.  Being willing to seek other people's advice is evidence of being a teachable Christian.  At times, God sends someone our way to provide clarity for us.  Being willing to listen is a powerful tool.

 

  1. Proverbs 2 states:  ¨My son, if you receive my words and treasure up my commandments with you, making your ear attentive to wisdom and inclining your heart to understanding; yes, if you call out for insight and raise your voice for understanding, if you seek it like silver and search for it as for hidden treasures, then you will understand the fear of the Lord and find the knowledge of God. ...¨  What joy we can find if we continue seeking wisdom and direction from God´s Word.

 

Being humble, seeking wisdom from the wise, and accepting correction are part of being teachable. Having a teachable spirit is evidence of allowing God's living and active Spirit to dwell within us:  Hebrews 4:12 states:  ¨For the word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soul and of spirit, of joints and of marrow, and discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart.¨
