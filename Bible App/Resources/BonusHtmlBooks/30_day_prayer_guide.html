30 Day Prayer Guide

 

Psalm 66:19 reads, “But truly God has listened; he has attended to the voice of my prayer.” When we pray, it is not just about asking God for things we need or desire. We should spend time communicating with Him the same way we communicate with our family and friends. To create a meaningful relationship with God, we should have conversations with Him that show our commitment and desire for a more personal relationship with Him. Our world is filled with confusion, worry, strife, and uncertainty, so this 30-day prayer guide is meant to help you create a more effective prayer life.

 

As you begin each day, we encourage you to find a quiet place where you can have some alone time with God.  In the same way that you might seek out an undisturbed location to catch up with an old friend, find somewhere for just you and God to be alone and enjoy each other’s company.

 

Week 1:  A Heart of Thankfulness

 

Day 1:  Thank God for who He is.  Have you ever given a just because gift?  Or, sent a note just to let someone know you were thinking about them?  Use today’s prayer time to let God know you are thinking about Him.  Thank Him for His role in your life and express your desire for more intimate time with Him. 

 

Day 2:  Thank God for who you are!  God created us in His image.  Each of us possesses talents and characteristics that make us different from anyone else.  As unique as your fingerprints are, so is your uniqueness in His eyes.  He created only one of you, and today is the day to thank Him for it.  Sometimes we fail to stop and look at our own abilities, but take some time today in prayer to reflect on who you are in Christ!

 

Day 3:  Thank God for the people closest to you.  As humans, we take people for granted.  We don’t always say ‘thank you’ or ‘you’re welcome’, but even more than that, we sometimes say it habitually.  Look at the people in your life and spend some time with God today thanking Him for their role in your life.  Ask Him to bless them and to help with any needs they may have.  Use this prayer time to reflect on how He has made sure that your life is easier because of the people He has surrounded you with.

 

Day 4:  Thank God for your success.  When was the last time you thanked God for your job?  Your health?  Your family?  When was the last time you truly reconciled your success to a divine Creator who wants only the best for His children?  We are sinners saved by His grace and blessed by His favor, so allow today’s prayer to focus on sending prayers of rejoicing for His role in bringing success to your life.  If you are currently feeling challenged and unable to see success, ask God for direction and wisdom to clarify if your definition of success needs clarifying or if you need to take direct action to claim the success He has waiting for you.  

 

Day 5:  Thank God for adversity.  Look back on your life and pick out some challenge or struggle that you faced. Pray about that struggle and ask God to help you understand what you learned from it.  If you already know how that adversity made you a better person, offer up thanksgiving for the strength and character building that came through His willingness to shape you.  If you find yourself currently struggling, spend some time praying for direction and wisdom.  Ask God to help you through the pain and to provide fruit as a result of this burden.

 

Day 6:  Thank God for your blessings.  There are so many blessings surrounding us.  Most of us reading this can list item after item that we do not need; it may be important to thank God for those items, but what are you truly grateful for?  Use this time to express your gratitude.  God loves for us to have a grateful heart and today is about acknowledging the way your life is an illustration of His favor. 

 

Day 7:  Offer up God’s many titles as reprise and praise.  Pray through the titles that we read in God’s word to acknowledge Him as the Supreme Being, the Alpha and Omega, the great I am….allow this time to be filled with rejoicing and thanksgiving. 

 

Week 2: Pray for our World

 

Day 8:  Pray for your family.  Maybe there is a special need in your family.  You need God’s intervention or response in some way.  This could be a physical need or a spiritual need.  Is there someone in your family who needs to receive Christ?  Pray for them.  Are you in need of some necessity that you have been unable to obtain?  God wants to hear our needs. 

 

Day 9:  Pray for your community.  All around us, people’s lives are changing.  The communities where we reside are also changing.  Is there a need in your community?  Are you aware of a specific problem?  Use today to ask God to bless your community in a specific way.

 

Day 10:  Pray for your state.  Throughout the country, state leaders are having to make tough choices.  Pray for guidance, direction, and clarity.  Pray for your leaders’ relationship with God and that the state where you reside would be led according to God’s leadership and Divine Appointment.

 

Day 11:  Pray for your country.  The country’s leaders are tasked with leading the citizens through a global pandemic.  Now more than ever, our leaders need insight, clarity, and wisdom from God.  Pray that their focus would be on seeking His input as they lead and make critical decisions. Put aside any personal feelings that politics may bring and use this time to express unbiased prayer for the leaders currently in office.

 

Day 12:  Pray for your world.  Across seven continents, billions of people are living with varying degrees of problems and calamity.  Pray that God would heal our land and guide our people back to Him.  Pray for a worldwide revival that will lead people to Christ in unprecedented numbers. 


Day 13:  Pray for peace.  In our homes, our communities, our states, our countries, and our world, we need peace.  Pray that God would soften the hearts of dictators, convict the hearts of unbelievers, and calm waves of violence in these varying places. 

 

Day 14:  Praise Him for the freedom to pray.  This past week has been focused on prayer for different geographical areas, some closer and more personal, while others are farther away, but all of them are within God’s reach.  Let today be a prayer of praise thanking God for the freedom you have to pray.  Ask Him to reveal Himself to you and to strengthen your relationship with Him through the intercessory prayers that you have brought on behalf of yourself and others. 

 

Week 3:  Strengthening Your Character

 

Day 15:  Pray for perseverance.  Maybe there is a challenge you are already going through or maybe you know of something coming up.  Use this day to pray for perseverance.  Pray that you will be refined and ready to stand in Christ when challenges arise. 

 

Day 16:  Pray for trust.  Do you trust easily?  Are there people you need to trust?  Proverbs 3:5-6 reminds us that God wants us to trust Him:  "Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight." Pray that even when you do not understand the day’s events, that even then you are able and willing to trust in the Lord.

 

Day 17:  Pray for optimism and proper perspective.   Our perspective has a huge impact on our days.  It also impacts the people around us whom we encounter directly and indirectly.  Ask God to shape your attitude and viewpoint to make you a positive person who reflects Christ’s love and hope. 

 

Day 18:  Pray for compassion.  There are so many people who need compassion.  This could be a family down the street; a homeless person whom you passed; a village on the other side of the world - as Christians, we are called to be compassionate individuals.  Use today to pray that your eyes would be opened to people in your path who need you to be more compassionate. 

 

Day 19:  Pray for a heart of gratitude.  The Lord wants us to have a thankful heart.  Ephesians 5:20 says, "Always giving thanks to God the Father for everything, in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ."   Ask Him to show you areas where you need to display more gratitude. 

 

Day 20:  Pray for self-control.  Is there an area in your life that you need help controlling?  This may come in major addictions or something less severe, but ask God to direct your steps.  If you need help, pray that God will send someone to lead you to the right resources.  Seek God’s plan and purpose in growing your self-control. 

 

Day 21:  Praise Him for His Unchanging and Omnipotent Role in your Life.  Allow today to be a prayer focused on praising Him.  Psalm 150:1 reminds us to Praise the Lord! 

 

 

Week 4:  Dig Deeper

 

Day 22:  Pray for clarity.  Allow today to be a prayer for clarity.  Maybe there is an upcoming decision or someone in your life who needs clarity.  Ask God to show you how to dig deeper into His Word and your Christian walk. 

 

Day 23:  Pray for forgiveness.  Ephesians 4:32: “Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you.”  Today’s prayer focus may be one of the harder prayer times, but it may also be one of the most important.  Who do you need to forgive?  Who is waiting on you to forgive them?  Pray about forgiveness and ask God to give you the strength and wisdom to follow through with what He shows you. 

 

Day 24:  Pray for purpose.  Allow this Bible verse to be the focus for today’s prayer time.  Seek how your purpose can be redefined or may be lacking.  Pray that the Lord will show you how to trust Him in all situations as you follow the paths His purpose leads you to.  Romans 8:28 says: “And we know that for those who love God all things work together for good, for those who are called according to his purpose.”

 

Day 25:  Pray for the lost.  There are so many unsaved people in the world.  Pray that God would convict the hearts of the lost and bring opportunities to share the Gospel with these individuals.  Maybe there is a specific person whom you should pray for or a specific population you are burdened for.  Ask God to give you a burden for the lost and to equip you with what you need to do His work. 

 

Day 26:  Pray for opportunity.  Maybe God has something new planned for you.  Perhaps, there is some new opportunity at church, in the community, or at work that has made you apprehensive.  Use today’s prayer time to seek clarity about current or upcoming opportunities. 

 

Day 27: Pray for growth.  Pray that you will remain focused on God’s purpose for your life.  Ask Him to show you new ways to grow.  Pray that as He stretches you into new territory that you will seek Him and be willing to commit to and follow His plan. 

 

Day 28:  Pray for unity.  Our communities, states, countries, and world need unity.  You may have a specific prayer for unity or a more general one that is definitely needed for our world. 

 

 

Ongoing:  Make Prayer a Daily Dialogue with Your Heavenly Father

 

Days 29 & 30.  On days 29 and 30, pray about which of the previous topics you should revisit.  Spend time praising Him and communicating with Him about how your relationship has been strengthened as a result of the intentional and focused prayer time that you’ve experienced over the previous four weeks. Commit to continued conversations with Him!   Make prayer a daily dialogue to grow, challenge, strengthen, and refine your relationship with Jesus Christ.

 

 

We hope that by creating a more effective prayer life, your relationship with Christ will be strengthened and your burdens will be lighter in knowing that we really do have a companion who is always with us.
