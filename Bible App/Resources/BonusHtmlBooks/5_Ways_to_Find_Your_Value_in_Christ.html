5 Ways to Find Your Value in Christ

 

Past hurt, current struggles, or looking to the wrong places for validation - all of these incidents can cause us to devalue ourselves. What is our value and how do we find our value in Christ? 

 

  1. God Considers Each Hair on Your Head as Special.  When was the last time you truly paid attention to losing a hair on your head?  This may seem cliche, but God tells us that even the hairs on our head are numbered.  How cool to think that Luke 12:7 reads, “But even the very hairs of your head are all numbered. Fear not therefore: ye are of more value than many sparrows.”  If God is so aware of how many hairs are on our head,  how valuable are we to Him?  We must look at ourselves the way He sees us - our value does not change based on our successes or failures.  God loves us the same regardless of what we do. 

 

  1. God made us in His image.  With all the creativity and genius mind of our God, think about what He could have made humans like, but He chose to make us in His image.  Hw wanted to share that intimate connection with us.  In Genesis 1:26-27, the Word reads: “So God created man in His own image; in the image of God He created him; male and female He created them.”

 

  1. You are Wonderfully Made! How incredible is that the same God who created the Heavens and the Earth though the world needed one of you too?  “For you created my inmost being; you knit me together in my mother’s womb” (Psalm 139:13).  God chose to create each one of us.  That alone makes us far more valuable than we will ever recognize if we look to other people for validation. 

 

  1. Stop Seeking Man’s Approval.  It is so easy for us to get caught up in what other people think of us. To truly find your value in Christ, look at what the Bible says in Galatians 1:10:  “For am I now seeking the approval of man, or of God?  Or am I trying to please man? If I were still trying to please man, I would not be a servant of Christ.” 

 

  1. Stop the Negative Self-Talk. Have you ever heard someone you care deeply about criticize or put themselves down?  That type of talk is usually matched with a reminder of how wonderful the person is.  We are quick to speak up and point out the strengths of others.  Hebrews 10:35 states:  “Therefore, do not throw away your confidence, which has a great reward.”  This verse tells us to remain self-confident. 

 

The world offers many reasons for us to look down on ourselves or to make comparisons.  Let’s start looking to God for our value.  He has given us the ultimate worth in His image and through His eyes!
