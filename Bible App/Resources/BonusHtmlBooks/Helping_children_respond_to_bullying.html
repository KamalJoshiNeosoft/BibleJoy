Helping Children Respond to Bullying

 

One of the most painful things we face as parents is when our children are hurting. All too often, this hurt is the result of bullying. Bullying can occur anywhere and can be defined as repeated offenses to an individual. These can be in the form of physical attacks, harsh words exchanged aloud or via text messages, or some other behaviors that seek to taunt and ridicule someone. What does the Bible say about responding to bullying?

 

            While the word ‘bullying’ is nowhere in the Bible, there are several key verses that remind us how to treat other people and where to turn to help. 

 

            Follow these helpful tips to direct your child on how to respond to bullying:

 

  1. Be strong.  2 Timothy 1:7 states: “For God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control.”  In this verse, we see God’s reminder that we are supposed to be strong.  Now, it can be hard when bullying is attacking our very identity, but even that is rooted in Christ and His love. 

 

  1. Take the high road.  Romans 8:37 states: “No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us.”  Winning with a bully is simply that we don’t need to attempt.  Rather than looking at how to win, try to lift the person up in prayer.  This can be so hard, but doing so could render eternal results. 

 

  1. God will meet our needs.  “And my God will supply every need of yours according to his riches in glory in Christ Jesus.”  It is definitely hard to see bullying as a need, but maybe growing more assertive or learning when to reach out and help someone else is a by-product of being bullied.  Even during adversity, God is with us, and it could be that He is growing up to do bigger things for Him during this difficult season. 

 

  1. Be a witness.  When our kids are hurting, we want to lash out, retaliate, and fix things, but this is not always possible.  Instead be a calm source of encouragement, wisdom, and redirection to the cross.  Romans 8:16 says, “The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit that we children of God.”  As His children, we have a duty to direct others to Christ.  Even during these trying times, remain faithful in prayer. 

 

  1. Seek His promise of protection.  Fixing bullying is simply not something that can be easily done.  Sure, steps must be taken to report bullying, but don’t let the other people involved become the primary focus.  Seek Him, and follow David’s wisdom when He says to see the Lord while he may be found.

 

The Lord will not allow the righteous to be shaken.  Use this difficult time to teach your child the proper way to deal with conflict.  In other words: pray, report bullying to the proper authorities, maintain a calm attitude, and redirect your child to positive areas in their life.
