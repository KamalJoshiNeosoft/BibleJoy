Helping Your Child Find Their Identity in Christ

 

The world tries hard to throw a lot at our children. Having a strong identity in Christ can help children remain focused on the end prize, eternity in Heaven. This identity can also help children say no to temptations that may come their way.

 

How can you help your children know who they are in Christ?

 

  1. Read the Bible with them.  Studying the Bible with your child will help them to see that it is so much more than just a book.  It is a roap map to provide guidance, direction, and examples of how to live life.  Each study session will bring them closer to God and farther away from the world. 

 

  1. Pray over them.  Choose a verse and let that be your prayer for them as they grow and develop into young adults.  Consider one of these verses as your prayer: 

 

    1. Ephesians 2:10:  ¨For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand, that we should walk in them.

 

    1. 1 Peter 2:9:  ¨But you are a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own possession, that you may proclaim the excellencies of him who called you out of darkness into his marvelous light.¨

 

  1. Remind them that you are there for support.  This world will attempt to throw a lot of stuff at our children.  If we have an open relationship with them, they are more open to discussing their problems and concerns with us.  These times allow us to direct them to God´s word and away from the world.  During these talks point them to verses including Ephesians 4:24: ¨And to put on the new self, created after the likeness of God in true righteousness and holiness.¨

 

  1. Don't try to do it all alone.  Look to other trusted adults to pour into your children.  These other adults can help your child build their identity in Christ.  They will see evidence in their lives and it will be another reinforcement about how finding our identity in Him is a wonderful way to stay on the straight and narrow path. 

 

  1. Pray with your child.  Praying with your child is a powerful change to grow together and strengthen your bond, as well as your relationship with God.  Having a set prayer time is wonderful, but also take the opportunity to pray with them whenever the need arises to seek guidance or to offer up praise. 

 

  1. Help them memorize/post key scriptures.  The task of helping your child understand and appreciate their identity in Christ may seem overwhelming, but focus on empowering them with scripture.  Help them write out key Bible verses and post those verses in visible places.  Likewise, you can work with your child to help them memorize scriptures such as Jeremiah 1:5: ¨Before I formed you in the womb I knew you, and before you were born I consecrated you; I appointed you a prophet to the nations.¨ 

 

Make your goal to empower your child with a reflection of Christ´s love and presence in their lives.  Help them understand His omnipotence and unconditional love which will ground them in their spiritual identity.
