7 ways to silence the doubt

 

Doubt is a feeling that the enemy uses to distract us. He fills our minds with doubts to get us off track. What can be done to silence the doubt?

 

  1. Stay grounded in God’s word. Read His word regularly. Start your day with His Word.  Use this early morning Bible time as a means to get equipped for the day. Matthew 21:21 states:  ¨And Jesus answered them, “Truly, I say to you, if you have faith and do not doubt, you will not only do what has been done to the fig tree, but even if you say to this mountain, ‘Be taken up and thrown into the sea,’ it will happen.¨

 

  1. Post scripture affirming your talents.  God has blessed each of us with unique talents.  Seek confirmation of your spiritual gifts.  Place reminders around your home, office, and even in your car that remind you who you are in Him!  These Bible verses will be the strength you need to silence the doubt when the enemy starts echoing those familiar words of doubt. 2 Corinthians 5:17:  ¨Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come.¨

 

  1. Seek guidance from others.  Sometimes we get so downtrodden that we have a hard time asking others for help.  The enemy wants to isolate us.  When he is able to isolate us, there is no one to pour back into us the reminders that God is able.  Don’t go through difficult times alone.  Find a trustworthy Christian whom you can share your concerns with.  Reach out to them when you need prayer. Hebrews 10:25 reminds us:  ¨Not neglecting to meet together, as is the habit of some, but encouraging one another, and all the more as you see the Day drawing near.¨

 

  1. Get involved.  Find a local shelter, your church, and/or another Christian organization that needs volunteers.  Devoting your time to helping others is a great way to build momentum and reduce doubt.  The more we can do for God’s Kingdom, the less time and space there will be for us to be attacked by the enemy.  In truth, his efforts may rev up as your volunteering goes into higher levels of commitment, but stay focused on God’s purpose.

 

  1. Keep a prayer journal.  Write down the areas the enemy uses to attack you.  Then find scripture to denounce that attack.  Record God’s response to the enemy’s lies.  Keep this record as solid proof of how much God values you and your purpose.

 

  1. Branch out.  Look for new ways to serve Him.  Look for people whom you can share the gospel with.  Be more intentional in His Word. 

 

  1. Pray without ceasing.  This familiar verse is so powerful.  Thankfully a prayer can be whispered in a business meeting the same way it can be shouted in a church service.  Don’t ever be afraid to pray to God.  He stands waiting and willing to listen to you.  You are His Child, and He always has time for you.

These tips will help you to silence the doubt.  By silencing the doubt, you will be able to live a more vibrant life that is focused on and in God's Holy Word.
