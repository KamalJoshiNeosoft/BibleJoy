Tips for Dealing with Anxiety

 

The uncertainty of today’s problems has created a lot of anxiety for people. Our daily lives are altered and those of us who are planners are unable to create a clear plan for the future because of so much change. These realities are generating anxiety in people who may have never felt the unexplainable fear and tension that comes from anxiety. Even with so much uncertainty, God’s Word provides us with some practical wisdom to apply during the most difficult of times.

 

  1. Be Still!  These two words are powerful.  There are multiple places in the Bible that God reminds us to simply be still.  In Psalm 46:10, His Word says:  "Be still, and know that I am God; I will be exalted among the nations, I will be exalted in the earth."  As humans, we want to fix things - we seek answers and try to apply solutions to the day’s problems, but when we do this, we are not allowing ourselves to let God take the reins.  Contrary to this, when we remember that “the Lord will fight for you; you need only to be still” (Exodus 14:14), we are surrendering our lives, our problems, and our anxiety to Him.  Rather than rushing about in haste and trying to remedy conditions that we really cannot change, we need to apply Psalm 37:7 and know that He will make a way:  “Be still before the Lord and wait patiently for him; fret not yourself over the one who prospers in his way, over the man who carries out evil devices!”  When anxiety is controlling your thoughts, emotions, and driving your fears, find something in nature that reminds you of God - a favorite tree, a beautiful flower, or even the wind - let this image of His faithfulness remind you of how His control far exceeds any anxieties we may have. 

 

  1. Talk to God.  In nature, I find it is easier to talk with God.  The daily distractions from manmade things are removed, and there is a natural tendency to be more open with the Creator.  It is during these times that I find it easiest to confess my anxieties and worries to Him.  He does not want us to be anxious and tells us in Phillippians 4:6-7 that we should come before Him in prayer and petition presenting our requests to Him.   Find a place where you can pray and allow His peace to become your driving force.

 

  1. Trade Worry for Wisdom. Anxiety and worry seem to go hand in hand, but how often does worry alter a situation?  If we are honest, we know that it only adds stress to our day and compounds our anxiety; nonetheless, look inside God’s Word and see how He led people through perilous times when they too were worried.  The Holy Bible is filled with wisdom about how to deal with life’s pressures and when we seek His wisdom, we are reminded that with it comes unconditional love and omnipresent companionship.
