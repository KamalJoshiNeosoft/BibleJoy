3 Lies the Enemy Wants You to Believe

 

Romans 5:8 states,¨But God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.¨How amazing is this verse? Jesus Christ loved each of us reading this enough to die for us before we were ever born. Before we ever set foot on this Earth, He had already made a way for us to spend eternity in Heaven with Him. This affirmation should be enough to dismiss the lies that the enemy so cunningly speaks. There are many lies he wants us to believe, but we have to take root in God's Word so we do not listen to these lies.

 

Let's take a look at five lies he wants us to fall for.  More importantly, let's look at what God says about these lies.

 

  1. Lie #1:  You are NOT enough.  How many times have you thought that you were not enough?  You let the lies creep in and start to focus on how much better of a father/mother, sister/brother, aunt/uncle, child, friend, and the list goes on and on that you should be.  Friend, until we make it home to Heaven, there will always be room for improvement, but God has said that you are enough!  2 Corinthians 12:9 states, ¨But he said to me, “My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.” Therefore I will boast all the more gladly of my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may rest upon me.¨  During the times when you feel like less, lean on Him more.  He wants to be your strength, your shield, and your comfort.  Friend, you are enough!  God says so! 

 

  1. Lie #2:  You have made too many mistakes.  Regardless of your past sin, we are all sinners.  When we accept Jesus Christ as our Lord and Savior, we become sinners saved by grace.  You have not made too many mistakes for Christ to clean you up, change you, and make you part of His Kingdom.  He paid the ultimate price because you are enough!  Matthew 10:31 states, ¨Fear not, therefore; you are of more value than many sparrows.¨  When the enemy steps up in your face, remind him who you are and that you are forgiven.  Your past sins are forgiven.  Psalms 103 reminds us that our sins are forgiven as far as the East is from the West. 

 

  1. Lie #3:  You do not have any talents.  God blessed each of us with unique talents.  Some of us are teachers, some are great speakers.  Some of us are able to show compassion for those in need while others are better suited for working in the background organizing and preparing others to go out and do on site work.  The statement ´God does not call the equipped, he equips the called is so true.  You will have whatever you need when He calls you.  1 Peter 4:10 states, ¨As each has received a gift, use it to serve one another, as good stewards of God's varied grace¨.  Furthermore, Romans 12:6 reads:  Having gifts that differ according to the grace given to us, let us use them: if prophecy, in proportion to our faith.¨  Pray without ceasing.  Seek His will, HIs purpose, and where He wants you to be.  When you find your place, you will find that your talents are great. 

 

There are times when we are downcast and the record of doubt seems to be stuck on replay.  Don't listen to it.  Don´t believe those lies.  You were bought with a price.  A price that is far more valuable than anything we can obtain in this world.  Seek your value in HIm and know that God's truth and many blessings do not align with the lies that the enemy tries to bombard you with. 

 

You are a chosen one!  You can do all things through Him!  Go be great today, and know that You are exactly who He meant for you to be!
