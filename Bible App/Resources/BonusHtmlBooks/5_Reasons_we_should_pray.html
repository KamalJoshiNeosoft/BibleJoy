5 Reasons We Should Pray

 

Communication is an essential part of any relationship. When we have news to share or need advice, we reach out to friends. Our dearest friend is our Heavenly Father. Prayer is the means by which we communicate with Him. Prayer should be as natural as the air we breathe.

 

  1. Pray to worship God.Exodus 3:14 states: ¨I Am that I Am….¨ In this verse, we see one of the dozens of names given to our God.  Prayer is a means to worship and adore Him.  Sometimes when out in nature and surrounded only by the world He made, I cannot help but offer up a prayer of worship.  He is so worthy of our praise and prayer, a means by which to communicate that praise to Him.

 

  1. Pray to offer thanks.  Philippians 4:6-7:  ¨Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God.¨  When we get a gift from a friend, we find a way to express our gratitude.  Praying to God is a means by which to say thanksgiving for the many ways He blesses us.  In reality, constant thanksgiving to God Above should be as steady at the beat of our hearts. 

 

  1. Pray to seek His advice. It can be hard for us to seek advice from our Heavenly Father instead of reaching out to an earthly friend because we think we need to hear an audible voice.  God speaks to us in so many ways that we sometimes do not take the time to discern.  Through prayer and adherence to His voice, we can seek His advice and direction.  Hebrews 4:12 states, ¨For the word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soul and of spirit, of joints and of marrow, and discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart.¨  It is this verse and many others that allow us to see that communication with God is as unique as the unconditional love He offers to us.

 

  1. Prayer strengthens us.  Afternoons spent over coffee, early morning breakfasts, long walks at dusk - all of these examples indicate times we might spend enjoying a friend´s company.  These times build memories and strengthen our bonds.  Prayer strengthens our bond with God, but it also strengthens us.  Through prayer, our souls are fed and our minds become more focused on Heaven instead of Earth. 

 

  1. Prayer restores us.  Romans 8:26 says, ¨Likewise the Spirit helps us in our weakness. For we do not know what to pray for as we ought, but the Spirit himself intercedes for us with groanings too deep for words.¨  When our pain is too great or our focus is too fogged, God knows our hearts.  He can interpret our groanings.  He made us.  Through that, the deep wounds become points of healing by which we can offer them up to God.  Allowing the private parts of ourselves to be open to healing brings renewal and strength.
