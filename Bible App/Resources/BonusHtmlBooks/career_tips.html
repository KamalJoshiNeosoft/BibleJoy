Career Tips(especially during these times)

 

More people than ever before are unemployed. The world is looking for hope, answers, and solutions that may seem overwhelming.  Even during a time when so many people are unemployed, God’s promise to help us is authentic and very real. 

 

These career tips may seem simple, but God provides us with tools to lead us where He directs.  We must only be willing to listen.

 

  1. Pray through your situation.  Are you unemployed?  Employed in a job that you took only because there was nothing else available?  Are you suddenly finding yourself unfulfilled in your job or sick of working from home?  God does not promise that we will get exactly what we pray for, but as Christians, we are called to a life of prayer.  Lift your burdens up to the Lord and watch Him work.  It may not be in that hour or even that day, but His word promises in Luke 12:24:  “Consider the ravens: they neither sow nor reap, they have neither storehouse nor barn, and yet God feeds them. Of how much more value are you than the birds!”

 

  1. Practice flexibility.  Many people have one specific type of job in mind that they want to pursue.  During today’s world, it may be necessary to explore new paths. Do not limit yourself and possibly cancel out potential opportunities. 

 

  1. Reconnect with contacts.  If there are past references or contacts you have that may lead to open doors, prayerfully consider these contacts.  Reach out and see if there are any possibilities that these resources may be able to provide.

 

  1. Be patient.  It is difficult to be patient when things are not happening as quickly as we would like or financial burdens are overwhelming us.  Romans 12:12 reminds us to “Be joyful in hope, patient in affliction, faithful in prayer.” 

 

  1. Ask for help.  Maybe you have been at the same job for years and are unsure of where to look.  Technology has changed the application process at many places of business.  Do not be afraid to ask for help.  Seeking answers about where to look for jobs or how to apply can be simplified when we tap into other resources. 

 

  1. Don’t limit yourself.  Look at online job postings, but also look for local advertisements via newspapers or other publications.  Be vigilant in your search and while you are waiting, focus on creating a new resume. 

 

Careers come and go, but God’s love and provision are unwavering.  His support and promise to never leave us nor forsake us is unconditional.  Through the ups and down of life, He will make a way for us.  Seek His will for your life in all areas.
